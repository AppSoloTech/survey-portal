## Findings

1. Medium — Guard workspace mutation results against URL changes.

   `SurveyWorkspaceLayout` correctly guards stale survey fetch responses with
   `isActive`, but `runSurveyMutation` has no equivalent guard. If an admin
   starts a save/publish/delete request on `/admin/surveys/1/...` and navigates
   to `/admin/surveys/2/...` before that request resolves, the old mutation can
   later call `setSurvey(response.survey)` and `setNotice(successMessage)` under
   the new URL. In the worst ordering, survey 1 renders inside the survey 2
   workspace until the next load or mutation corrects it.

   Affected code:
   - `apps/web/src/pages/admin/SurveyWorkspaceLayout.tsx`, lines 81-103
   - related route-owned survey identity: `apps/web/src/pages/admin/SurveyWorkspaceLayout.tsx`, lines 36-44

   Suggested fix:
   Track the active `surveyId` in a ref or include an expected survey id in
   `runSurveyMutation`, and only apply `setSurvey`, `setNotice`, `setError`,
   and `setIsSubmitting(false)` if the layout is still on the same survey. This
   should mirror the existing stale-response protection in the fetch effect.
